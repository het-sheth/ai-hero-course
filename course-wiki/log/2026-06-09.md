## Day 1 · Fundamentals (started)

Opened Module 03 with the two foundational lessons:

- [[day-1-fundamentals/constraints-of-llms|The Constraints of LLMs]] (03.01) — the four constraints: smart zone vs dumb zone (attention scales quadratically; smart zone ≈ first 80–100k tokens regardless of the 1M window), the fuzzy-JPEG database (why hallucinations happen), statelessness (every clear wipes memory → codebase quality matters), and knowledge cutoff.
- [[day-1-fundamentals/subagents|Subagents]] (03.02) — context-saving delegation: the orchestrator spawns a fresh-context subagent for focused work (e.g. exploration, sometimes on a cheaper model) and gets back just a summary, keeping the main window clean for implementation.
- [[day-1-fundamentals/codebase-exploration|Codebase Exploration]] (03.03) — exploration is foundational (LLMs are stateless); a generic prompt does a shallow ~6-file skim, but putting the word **"explore"** in the prompt triggers a dedicated Explore subagent that aggressively reads files and returns a rich summary. Covers both the Problem (exercise) and Solution (walkthrough) sub-pages.
